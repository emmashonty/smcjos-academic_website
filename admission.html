Admission Instructions and Requirements

St. murumba operates both the junior and senior secondary schools in accordance with the national policy on Education. Admission into the school is based on certain criteria: -

As a state grant-aided school, the policy of 90% non-indigenes is being implemented. However, over the years, it has been noticed that indigenes have not been able to fully utilize their quota. A student seeking admission into junior secondary school is expected to pass the collage’s entrance examination as well as the interview test.

Though a catholic school, admission is given to all categories of people that satisfy the admission criteria. To gain admission into the senior secondary school, a candidate is expected to have passed his JSCE in his junior secondary school. Passes in math’s and English are compulsory. The state government dose placement also into senior secondary schools in the state.

Admission can be gained into other classes if and when there are vacancies. A transfer examination will then be conducted and admission is based strictly on merit.

ABOUT ST MURUMBA COLLEGE HISTORY

St. Murumba collage then called blessed murumba collage came into existence in1959. The school is rated of the top secondary school in the whole of plateau state. the school was established out of the burning desires by the catholic community in Jos to see to the advanced education of her young boys who were graduating from elementary school. Many top personnel in the society today are products of the St. murumba collage.

ST MURUMBA COLLEGE HISTORY

11TH of July 1958 an application was sent to the permanent secretary, Ministry of Education Kaduna applying for the approval to establish a Roman Catholic Mission Secondary Day School for Boys in Jos. The application was approved and on 27th of January 1959 the operation of the school commenced.

After first classes were held in Fatima school due to the lack of sufficient building on the site of the school. The institution was called Blessed Murumba College at that time the proprietor was Most Rev. Dr Reddington. The institution started with the admission of 30 students.

The very first Principal was Rev. Fr. John Hacket followed by very Rev. Fr. Jeremiah O’Comel, afterwards Mr. Sati Christopher Dalyop served as the Principal of the institution but not for a long time. Rev. Fr. Benedict Obichlegwu served as the Principal of the institution when it was renamed St. Murumba College from 1980 – 2000. Thereafter Fr. Anthony Enkela served from 2000 - 2007, Rev. Fr. Cyril Deshi served from 2007 – 2011 followed by Rev. Fr. Peter Uchu who served from 2011 – 2016 then Rev. Fr. Joseph Davou Choji commenced from 2016 and still serving.

The institution that kicked off as a pure day boys but presently its both day and boarding school. The institution is known to stand out in both academic performances, sport activities, music and politics.

We have the likes of Prof Tonie Okpe of set 1973 who is considered as one of the pioneer academicians this noble institution has produced. He was the Head of Department Fine Art ABU Zaria. Likewise the school produced a Winner Judo Champion all Africa Games in 1979 by Dr Fred Nyam. He also the first black man to win the Emperor Belt (Medal) in Japan. He holds a PHD in Computer Engineering and Telecom Expert, he is also a member in the Nigeria Judo coaching team Greece 2004 and in China 2008. The likes of P2 (Peter & Paul) are St Murumba Old boys who have excel in the world of music.

The institution also produced Priests. Very Rev. Martins Dama. Among them are Very Rev. Fr. John Dalyop, Very Rev. Fr. Hycint, Fr. Anthony Kangyep, Fr. Blaise Agwom and a synificant number of priest in other religious bodies.

In the sphere of politics, there is also the person of Mallam Isa Yuguda of set 1973 who was the former M.D Inland bank of Nigeria, Late senator G.N.S Pwajok and so many local government chairman.

The institution’s structural facilities started with about 3 structures; the administrative block at the centre and two other structures for the classrooms, but as time went on more structures was added, presently we have 11 standardised structures, which includes the administrative block, 6 blocks for the classrooms, including the library, 2 blocks for the hostel, a block for the kitchen and a store and the other block for the dining. Others are offices and science laboratories, a new ultra-modern two (2) storey Hostel is now at super-structure level.

The present serving Principal in person of Rev. Fr. Joseph Davou Choji is making sure the school is at its best.

The institution has always had the excellent, well qualified and trained teachers and staff to suit the need of the students. Presently, we have 34 academic staff, 26 non-academic staff and 3 corps members serving with the institution. Due to the crisis in 2001, the number of students dropped to about 200 as lots of families were killed and lots more relocated. The institution was able to manage its affairs. Now the school has been trying to grow in numbers and now the students are about 440 students and still growing.

FACILITIES AND FEATURES

The school provides the necessary facilities for learning. The following facilities have been beneficial in the teaching / learning process in the collage.

  • Twenty-three (23) large airy classroom for lessons
  • Two equipped technical workshops: - one for woodwork and the other for metalwork.

It is pertinent to note here that the school is better equipped than most secondary schools because technical equipment supplied by the state government was added to the one bought by the school. There are the fairly well- equipped labs for chemistry, physics and biology where practical are carried out by the senior students.

There is also geography arena, an administrative block, a large assembly hall, store and office for staff, a large playing field for all forms of sports, free quarters for teaching personnel, a well-equipped computer center as well as a well-furnished conference center.

School Fees

On admission parents are required to pay the entire 1st term school fees of N8,600.00 before resumption.

Subsequent fees will be less as first term fees are always huge to other charges. Further explanation are contained in the admission letter.

Uniform

The uniforms are provided by the school and parents must buy from the school to avoid different types and styles of sewing. Due to the federal government ban on importation of foreign material which is more qualitative, prizes of materials have skyrocketed. The school provides 3 pairs of short sleeve and shorts at the cost of 3,600.00

Other uniforms include:

  • One long sleeve cardigan N700.00
  • One short sleeve cardigan N650.00
  • One vest for the house/ sport N300.00

Text Book

Text books are very important tools for studies in the school and are thereby compulsory. Parents are required to obtain the list of textbooks from the school and purchase them before hand to equip the students properly.

RULES OF THE COLLAGE

  • School programme begins at 7.30 am ends at 4p.m. no student shall leave the school compound during this period without the permission of the principal/vice-principal/duty master.
  • All students must be at the assembly from Monday to Friday by 7.30am
  • Students shall wear their school uniforms within the school compound, except during games.
  • Uniform consist of white shirt on white shorts/ trouser on Mondays and light blue shirt on dark blue shorts/ trouser from Tuesday to Friday.
  • During class and study time, students shall not leave their classroom without the express permission of a teacher or their class prefect.
  • No student shall enter a classroom other than his own without the permission of a teacher or the class prefect.
  • Silence shall always be observed in the classroom during study and during class period in case a teacher is absent.
  • No student shall remain in his classroom during recreation periods unless permitted or ordered to do so by a member of staff for a special reason.
  • Silence shall be observed in the library so that students may read undisturbed. Any student seeking information should approach the librarian and enquire very quietly.
  • English shall be the language of the compound. Students therefore, shall not speak any other language while they are in the compound unless during vernacular lessons.
  • Bullying is not tolerated and such case will be severely dealt with.
